Raft Foundation Design Fresno CA – Geotechnical Solutions for Expansive Soils

Sound ground. Sound decisions.

LEARN MORE

The western edge of the San Joaquin Valley has some of the most aggressive subsidence rates on earth—nearly 28 feet since 1925 near Mendota, just west of Fresno. Combine that with the expansive Porterville and Exeter clay series mapped across the metro area and you have a foundation challenge that isolated footings simply cannot handle. Raft/mat foundation design becomes the rational engineering response. Our team applies site-specific soil parameters—swell pressure, elastic modulus, and seasonal moisture variation—to deliver a rigid mat that bridges soft zones, controls differential settlement, and withstands a design PGA of 0.35g per ASCE 7-22 for Fresno County. Before finalizing the raft geometry we often run CPT testing to map compressible layers without disturbing the sample, especially where the Corcoran clay member is present.

A properly designed raft foundation in Fresno must control both settlement from overdraft subsidence and heave from expansive clays—two forces acting in opposite directions.

Methodology and scope

IBC Chapter 18 and ACI 360R-10 govern mat foundation analysis in California. The Fresno building department enforces these strictly because of the variable alluvial deposits from the Kings and San Joaquin rivers. Our raft/mat foundation design process starts with a Winkler spring model calibrated to subgrade reaction modulus from field plate load tests or back-calculated from SPT N-values. We check punching shear at column perimeters, flexure in both directions, and long-term creep settlement using consolidation data from oedometer tests. For sites near the Friant-Kern Canal where groundwater fluctuates 8 to 15 feet seasonally, the buoyancy check is mandatory. Where the upper 10 feet show clay with PI above 35, we recommend pairing the mat with stone columns to reduce post-construction heave and improve bearing capacity before concrete placement.

Local considerations

Sites north of Herndon Avenue near the San Joaquin River floodplain behave differently than the hardpan areas east of Clovis Avenue. The northern zone has loose silty sand lenses that can liquefy under a M6.5+ event—our raft/mat foundation design for those parcels incorporates a densified subgrade or ground improvement to maintain bearing. The eastern benchlands sit on cemented alluvial deposits with shallow refusal around 4 to 6 feet, reducing settlement but increasing excavation cost. The downtown corridor has 15 to 30 feet of undocumented fill from early 20th-century construction, making borings essential. Skipping a detailed geotechnical investigation before locking in the mat geometry leads to two failure modes: edge lift heave during wet winters and center settlement during the irrigation season.

Frequently asked questions

How much does raft/mat foundation design cost in Fresno?

Engineering fees for a typical residential or light commercial raft foundation design in the Fresno area range from US$1,100 to US$3,920, depending on the complexity of the soil profile, the number of borings required, and whether ground improvement like overexcavation or stone columns is needed. A detailed proposal follows the initial site walk.

When is a raft foundation better than isolated footings in Fresno?

A raft makes sense when the allowable bearing capacity drops below 1,500 psf, when expansive clay with PI above 30 is present in the upper 10 feet, or when differential settlement between columns is expected to exceed 0.5 inches over 20 feet. Many Fresno lots check all three boxes.

Does a mat foundation eliminate the need for deep piles?

Not always. In areas west of Highway 99 where the Corcoran clay is 40-plus feet thick and heavily overconsolidated, a mat alone may not control total settlement. We evaluate pile-supported mats where the compressible layer is too deep to remove economically. The decision comes from the consolidation test data.

What is the typical timeline from investigation to stamped drawings?

Fieldwork takes 2 to 3 days for a standard parcel. Laboratory testing runs 10 to 14 days for consolidation and swell tests. Analysis and drafting of the stamped foundation plans take another 7 to 10 days. A full package is ready in 3 to 4 weeks from authorization.
